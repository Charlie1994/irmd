The 1st International Workshop on Indexing and Retrieval for Multimedia Database

The explosive growth of heterogeneous multimedia data (i.e., text, image, audio and video) on the Internet has brought great challenges on how to effectively and efficiently index, retrieve and manage these rich resources. It is imperative to develop new and efficient multimedia representation techniques, which can be easily performed with big multimedia data and achieve promising performance in the various multimedia applications.

In recent years, compact representation learning or hashing has attracted considerable research attentions, due to its advantages of high retrieval efficiency and extremely low storage cost. It has been well-recognized as an advanced tool for facilitating efficient large-scale multimedia applications. This workshop is devoted to the publication of high-quality research papers on binary representation learning for various large-scale multimedia applications, such as, multimedia retrieval, recommendation, question-answering, etc. The special issue will seek for original contribution of works, which address the key challenges and problems.

Research Interest

Topics of interest include but not limited to:

  • Compact representation learning for large-scale image/video/music/audio retrieval and indexing
  • Compact representation learning for large-scale multimedia recommendation/multimedia question-answering/semantic indexing
  • Compact representation learning for large-scale multimedia security
  • Compact representation learning for mobile-based multimedia applications
  • Binary representation learning for image/video classification/detection/tracking
  • Novel data-independent binary representation learning algorithms
  • Online hashing learning for streaming multimedia data
  • Robust hashing learning algorithms for noisy multimedia data
  • Distributed hashing learning for distributed multimedia data
  • Compact representation learning algorithms for accelerating large-scale multimedia analysis
  • New multimedia applications of compact representation learning
  • Survey papers with regards of compact multimedia representation learning
  • New benchmark datasets for compact multimedia representation learning

Submission Guidelines

The proceedings of the workshops will be published jointly with the conference proceedings and all papers must be formatted following the IEEE ICDE 2019 submission guidelines available at the conference webpage. We welcome research papers (full or short), vision papers, demo papers and industry papers showcasing graph analytics in real applications.

Including the bibliography and any possible appendences,

  • Full papers and vision papers should be a maximum of 8 pages in length
  • Short papers, demo papers and industry papers should be a maximum of 4 pages in length.
